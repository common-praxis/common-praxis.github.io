Synthesizing the abstract and the asphalt.

“A jack of all trades is a master of none, but oftentimes better than a master of one.”

In a world of hyper-specialization, we’ve lost the ability to see how the shape of our cities affects the state of our souls. Common Praxis is an attempt to zoom out.

It is a publication dedicated to the connective tissue between philosophy, policy, and the physical world.

Why “Common Praxis”?

Praxis is the process by which a theory, lesson, or skill is enacted, embodied, or realized. “Common” refers to both our shared spaces and our shared humanity.

This internet web page is where we move from “What is the good life?” to “How do we build a suburb that allows us to live it?”

Why Subscribe?

If you are tired of shallow takes and want a newsletter that treats you like a thinker, you’re in the right place. There is no niche here; only the pursuit of understanding how we can live better, together.

Common Praxis is run by Fatma Omer.
