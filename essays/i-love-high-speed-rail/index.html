I Love High Speed Rail and You Should Too

High Speed Rail Redefines Living, Working, and Saving the Planet

Common Praxis

High speed rail is often framed as a faster way to commute, but it has tremendous potential to be a foundational reset for how a modern society functions. In the status quo, we are trapped in a cycle of widening highways that immediately refill with traffic and enduring airport experiences that feel more like endurance tests than travel. High speed rail breaks this cycle by introducing a third way of movement that operates with the frequency of a subway but the range of a jet. High speed rail allows us to trade the chaotic, fragmented nature of fossil-fuel travel for a streamlined, electrified spine that supports every sector of human life.

The environmental argument for high speed rail has evolved from simple emission reductions into a sophisticated strategy for atmospheric restoration. We are currently facing the “catenary wall,” where the physics of aviation and heavy trucking simply cannot meet net zero targets for mid-range journeys. High speed rail is roughly 12 times more energy efficient than flying because it avoids the massive energy expenditure required for takeoff and landing. By migrating high volume routes to the tracks, we allow our skies to clear and reduce the urban heat island effect caused by massive asphalt highways and idling engines. High speed rails create networks that often double as energy corridors as they host renewable power generation along their tracks.

Economically, high speed rail acts as a massive spatial fix for global imbalances. Wealth and opportunity are often concentrated in a few superstar cities, leading to astronomical housing costs and a hollowed out middle class in regional areas. High speed rail creates time space compression, where a city 300 kilometers away becomes part of the local labor market. This allows for a productivity renaissance where workers can live in affordable, green, regional towns while accessing high value jobs in the urban core. This agglomeration effect ensures that ideas and innovation flow faster between connected hubs, turning isolated cities into a single, high octane economic engine.

The Canadian Context: A Winter-Proof Nation-Builder

In Canada, the Alto project, connecting the Quebec City–Windsor corridor, is uniquely positioned to solve Canadian-specific problems. While Canada is a vast country, 43% of its labor force is concentrated in this single 1,000-kilometer strip. High speed rail is uniquely suited to our climate; unlike highways and air travel, which are frequently crippled by whiteouts and ice storms, high speed rail utilizes dedicated, all-weather tracks that maintain high reliability during the harshest winters. This makes it a vital safety intervention. Winter stands out as the busiest period for crashes in Canada, and by providing a 300 km/h alternative to the treacherous Highway 401, it offers a safe-haven mode of transport that saves lives while keeping the economy moving regardless of the forecast.

Furthermore, Canadian high-speed rail is a tool for social reconciliation and regional survival. The Alto project is designed with a heavy emphasis on Indigenous involvement and transit-oriented development, aiming to build over 60,000 new residential units along the corridor. This is essential for tackling the Canadian housing crisis, as it turns commuter towns like Peterborough and Trois-Rivières into high-access hubs. By linking Ottawa, Montreal, and Toronto with 60-to-90-minute travel times, Canada can finally stop its brain drain from smaller cities to the few major hubs, allowing talent to flourish across the entire corridor while significantly reducing the load on the country’s aging and congested electrical and transport grids.

The hidden genius of rail travel is that it reclaims our think time. The stress of driving or the fragmented ritual of airport security drains our cognitive energy. A high-speed train provides long, unbroken blocks of time in a stable, quiet environment. This cognitive productivity boosts a nation’s GDP by maximizing the utility of its workforce. Beyond the numbers, HSR restores human scale to our cities. Stations are built in downtown cores, integrating with parks and cafes rather than parking lots. It is an investment in a future where we move faster, work smarter, and live more sustainably, proving that the best way to move forward is on a set of well engineered, high speed tracks.
